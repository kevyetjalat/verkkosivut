Wojtek, Happy Warrior

The name Wojtek is the diminutive form of "Wojciech" (Happy Warrior), an old Slavic name still common in Poland.

The Polish II Corps was reassigned to fight alongside the British Eighth Army in the Italian campaign. Regulations for the British transport ship forbade mascot and pet animals. To get around this restriction, Wojtek was officially drafted into the Polish Army as a private and listed among the soldiers of the 22nd Artillery Supply Company.

Wojtek helped his unit to convey ammunition by carrying 45kg crates of artillery shells. Wojtek carried boxes that normally required 4 men, which he would stack onto a truck or other ammunition boxes.

This service at Monte Cassino earned him promotion to the rank of corporal.

Wojtek copied the other soldiers, drinking beer, smoking and even marching alongside them on his
hind legs.

“To be defeated, but not to give in, is victory."
