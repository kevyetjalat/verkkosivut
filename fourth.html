Legacy of Wojtek

After the end of World War II in 1945, Wojtek was transported to Scotland, with the rest of the 22nd Company. They were stationed on Sunwick Farm, near the village of Hutton. Wojtek soon became popular among local civilians and the press, and the Polish-Scottish Association made him an honorary member.

Wojtek was given to Edinburgh Zoo, where he spent the rest of his life, often visited by journalists and former Polish soldiers, some of whom tossed cigarettes for him to eat, as he did during his time in the army.

Wojtek was a frequent guest on BBC television's Blue Peter programme for children.

The many memorials to the soldier-bear include a plaque in the Imperial War Museum in London as well as multiple monuments around Poland and the UK.

Wojtek died in December 1963, at the age of 21, weighing nearly 500 kilograms, and was over 1.8 metres tall.
